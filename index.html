Spaceflights that are launched into outer space can be categorized into orbital spaceflights and suborbital spaceflights.

Since 1957, there are more than 6,018 launch attempts for orbital spaceflights, which means the spaceflight is placed on a trajectory where it could remain in space for at least one orbit.

And 29,455 launch attempts for suborbital spaceflights, which means that the spaceflight will not complete one orbital revolution.

The following story will focus on orbital spaceflights.
